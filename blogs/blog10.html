MY REPRESENTATION AND COMMUNICATION STRATEGIES

The data visualization that I have decided to make interactive is my pie chart. My strategy for doing this is adding more data sets that the user can choose from, allowing the user to view a pie chart representing the different qualities of living in the city of their choosing. I have chosen to communicate the data sets through a pie chart since pie charts are simple and easy to understand in my opinion. The chart will clearly represent the score of each quality of living aspect of the different cities. The user will be able to switch between the cities using buttons seeing that this is the simplest way I could think of to create this interaction. This was however the original idea, adly I could not get it to work. Thus I settled for letting the user hover over the pie chart to make the legend appear.

Riche et al. in their book, Data Driven Storytelling, chapter 9 explains some strategies and good practices for communicating data to audiences. One of these good practices or strategies is having a consistent design approach, which refers to having a consistent style so that audiences or users to relate in a sense to the work which could encourage them to return. This consistent style refers to fonts, colours, layout, and types of interaction (Riche et al., 2018; pp. 219-220). This stood out to me and helped me focus my designs towards a single style and colour scheme. I will continue to do this in order to keep communication with users clear.

Evanthia Dimara, and Charles Perin in their paper, What is Interaction for Data Visualization, describe one aspect of good interaction that they have found through their research. This aspect is simple interaction over minimal information. They also state that good interaction is direct and simple in order to improve the user’s understanding of the data that is being represented (Dimara and Perin, 2020). What I understand by this is that when designing interaction with regards to data visualization one must keep in mind that overstimulating the user with too much power in terms of interaction or an over-abundance of information that will leave the user overwhelmed. It is for this reason why I focused my strategy of designing my interaction and communication on keeping everything simple and clear. This goes hand in hand I believe seeing that decreasing the amount or depth of you information would allow you to focus more on how the left over data (so to speak) is going to be represented.

Ultimately, I’m not going to give the user too much different interactions that might distract or overwhelm them, this allowed me to focus on simple representation of my data (each pie chart segment represents an aspect of the quality of life in Dar Es Salaam). The data set that I have chosen from the start have already been filtered by myself before implementing them into the data visualizations. The reason that I decided to do this was to simplify the visualizations and ensure that the amount of data that is being represented isn’t overwhelming and that the graphs aren’t littered with data that is mundane and uninteresting, thus creating simple but effective communication and representation.
