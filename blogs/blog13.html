REFLECTION ON DATA-DRIVEN ART

This blog post’s question was, “Critical reflection on the representation and communication strategies you used in your Data-Driven Artwork. Explain what you wanted to achieve and how you went about it.”, that being said; the brief for this assignment didn’t require me to make any data-driven art. Thus, I have decided to reflect on the data driven artwork that I have looked at in the previous blog post.

Every Playboy Centerfold, The Decades (normalized) (2002), by Jason Salavon is an art piece that was created by taking every Playboy centerfold over the decades in order to make a mean average of the decade’s centerfolds. I will not be adding a picture of this artwork seeing that I already did in the previous blog post.

Jason used pixel manipulation and averaging in order to represent every Playboy centerfold for the 1960’s, 1970’s, 1980’s, and the 1990’s. the strategy here was to average out the colour pixels of the centerfolds in order to create an average centerfold that would ultimately show how the models’ skin tone, hair colour, and body shape has changed over the years (possibly due to audience taste changing). This strategy, as explained in my previous blogpost, isn’t a new strategy seeing that it has been used often in facial recognition software, according to Viégas and Wattenberg.

Communication is a very important aspect of data visualization; thus, we can assume that it is just as important for data-driven artwork. According to Riche et al. in Data Driven Storytelling one great strategy for good communication is consistency. For this particular art piece, we can see that Jason manipulated the colour pixels in such a way that all of the Playboy centerfolds throughout the decades have a similar look and feel to them. For instance, the backgrounds of the pictures are all a blueish shade. This strategy results in models themselves standing out as well the changes in model type across the decades.

I believe that the point or commentary that Jason wanted to portray with this artwork was that people’s taste in women have changed rapidly between the 1960’s and the 1990’s, and that Playboy has picked their models accordingly. That being said I am can not possibly know whether this speculation is true or not, it is just my interpretation of the art. How Jason achieved this is by creating a mean average of the centerfolds while manipulating the colours in order to make the backgrounds seem similar, putting more emphasis on the differences between the averages between the four decades. To reiterate, these are merely how I understand the artist’s work with regards to his goals and strategies.
