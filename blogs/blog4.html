HISTORICAL DATA VISUALIZATION

Clive Thompson explains in The Surprising History of the Infographic that our society has become used to data visualization. If you, for example, go to any news website you will probably find infographics detailing political support to the various running parties (during election times). Thompson continues to explain that data visualization is not a new invention; in fact, people have been using imagery to cut through the dense data that they have to work with throughout history (Thompson, 2016).

Thompson explains that data visualization has created a new type of citizen; a citizen that is educated with statistical thinking. Clive provides a number of examples of data visualizations throughout history and the effect of this way of displaying information. It is quite clear that data visualization is an important aspect of society seeing that it can take massive amounts of data and display it in a way that blatantly points out underlying issues with certain subjects. Data visualization thus provide the needed data for certain important decisions/changes in such a way that anybody can see why the decisions/changes need to be made (Thompson, 2016). One can imagine that thanks to Nightingale it was a lot easier to take the appropriate steps in order to protect the soldiers or to prevent any more unnecessary deaths.

This is just one example of historical data visualization that blatantly shows the importance of presenting data in a way that anyone can understand it. Without creating digestible/usable information from the mass of raw data out there in the world; there would be no way of sharing statistics effectively with the public and it would take an immensely long time to make certain important (life or death) decisions or changes. Ultimately the importance of proper data visualization has always been obvious and data visualization can only help humanity moving forward.
