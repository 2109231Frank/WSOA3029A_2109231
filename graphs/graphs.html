REFLECTION ON MY GRAPHS

The strategy behind my graphs was aimed at sharing data in a simple and clear way. I did not want to overcomplicate my graph with fancy graphics or weird graph types. I decided on a bar chart and a pie chart almost immediately seeing that they ar the only graphs I have ever worked with in the past. That being said I do plan on broadening my experience by playing around with other graphs in the future. My graphs are indeed simple (which is what I wanted) but seem a little boring unfortunately

My representational strategy boils down to my goal to share my data in the simplest way possible. The orange colours have a nice contrast with the website’s background, thus making the charts clear and easy to read. I rounded the data to the nearest integer in order to make each of the bars stop on a number so that it is easier to mark at what height they are, this luckily did not have a hige impact on the pie chart since it works ith percentages. Kidd explained that representation is a very important part of communicating information and that it should embody the data that is being represented in such a way that it shapes the represented reality in the given frame (Kidd, 2015). I don’t think that my representation of the data is particularly interesting, but I do think it is somewhat effective seeing that the bars effectively show how positive or negative the various aspects of each city is while the segments of the pie chart clearly show which aspects of New York City are its best aspects. Looking at both the charts at the same time we clearly see the representation of the difference between both city’s quality of living. Ultimately, I believe that my representation was successful but could be much better moving forward.

The communication of my graphs basically just wanted to share the quality of various aspects of life within Johannesburg and New York City. I believe that my graph is successful in communicating which aspect of life each bar/segment represents and how good or bad that aspect is, however looking at my bar I cannot think of much else to say which just justifies my opinion that the graphs are boring and could be improved in future. Looking at the Communicating Data to an Audience reading we see that a infographic designer needs to keep two main questions in mind: “What does the audience know?”, and “What does the audience want?” (al., 2018). Keeping these in mind I decided which aspects I would include in my graphs (seeing that my API provides information on 17 different aspects of living in each city). I decided to include aspects that not only matter while living in each city, but also while just visiting them. I did this because I believe that people who do not live in these two cities would also be looking at the graphs and they would not exactly care about aspects such as: “Business Freedom”, “Start-ups”, or “Venture Capital”. I also thought that some of the most important aspects that audiences would want to know about were things like: “Safety”, “Commute”, “Tolerance”, and “Leisure & Culture”.

Ultimately, I feel that my charts are effective in communicating the information that I wanted to share. That being said I think that they could have been a bit more interesting if I could figure out how to use D3 more effectively. In future I will be sure to research more interesting ways to use D3 and SVG’s as well as look at more examples of data visualization.
